# NeuralCost Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

This GTM strategy focuses on a **Zero-Budget Acquisition Plan**, leveraging NeuralCost as a high-value lead magnet (PLG - Product Led Growth) to drive highly qualified leads into a premium consulting funnel.

## 1. Target Audience Profile (ICP)
- **Primary ICP**: CTOs, VP Engineering, or Founders at Seed to Series B startups (15-150 employees).
- **The Pain**: They authorized 5 different AI tools 6 months ago. Now, they have 40 engineers using a mix of Copilot, Cursor, ChatGPT, and Claude. Their AWS/API bills are spiking, and they have no central visibility.
- **The Hook**: "You are overpaying for AI by 30%. Find out exactly where in 60 seconds."

## 2. Product Hunt Launch Strategy
**Goal**: Top 3 Product of the Day. 300+ audits generated.
- **Tagline**: "Stop overpaying for AI. Audit your team's stack in 60 seconds."
- **First Comment**: A transparent founder story about how we audited a 50-person startup and found they were paying for Copilot ($19/mo) AND Cursor ($40/mo) for every engineer. We saved them $11k/yr.
- **Offer**: "Free detailed audit report + 15 min review call for the first 50 hunters."
- **Visuals**: Use the screenshot-worthy "Results Dashboard" showing the stacked bar chart and savings numbers.

## 3. Social & Content Growth (X / LinkedIn)
The strategy is **"Build in Public" meets "Financial Teardowns"**.
- **The Format**: Post anonymous, redacted teardowns of real startups.
  - *Example Tweet*: "Just audited a Series A startup's AI stack. 30 engineers. They were spending $2,400/mo. We got it down to $1,600/mo without losing a single capability. Here's exactly what we cut: 🧵"
- **The CTA**: "Drop a reply with your top 3 tools and seat counts, and I'll give you a 1-sentence optimization. Or run the math yourself at [Link]."

## 4. Reddit Guerrilla Marketing
Do NOT post direct links. Engage in high-intent conversations.
- **Target Subreddits**: `r/SaaS`, `r/startups`, `r/devops`, `r/experienceddevs`.
- **The Play**: Look for threads complaining about OpenAI API bills or asking "Is Cursor worth it if we have Copilot?".
- **The Response**: Provide a highly technical, math-backed answer. "We actually built an engine to calculate this exact overlap. If you have 10 devs, you're wasting $190/mo running both. I built a free tool that visualizes this if you're curious, but the short answer is: drop Copilot."

## 5. Cold Outreach (Outbound)
Targeting operations managers and fractional CFOs.
- **Subject**: Your engineers' AI stack
- **Body**: "Hi [Name], noticed [Company] recently crossed 50 employees. Most teams your size are accidentally double-paying for AI tools (usually Copilot + Cursor overlap). I built a free calculator that takes 60 seconds to find redundant AI spend. Check it out here: [Link]. If it finds >$500 in waste, I'd love to help you fix it."
